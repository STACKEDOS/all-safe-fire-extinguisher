Our Story

All Safe Fire Extinguisher Co., Inc. was founded on June 1, 1981, in Temecula, California. From the first day of business, the company was built around a single service: portable fire extinguishers. Inspection, recharging, hydrostatic testing, sales, and installation. That is what we did in 1981. That is what we do today.

We have never expanded into fire alarms. We have never taken on sprinkler systems. We have never dabbled in suppression systems or other fire protection trades. Every dollar we have earned, every technician we have trained, and every relationship we have built over 45 years has been rooted in one specialty. That is not an accident. It is a deliberate choice, and we believe it is why the largest property management companies in the country trust us with their properties.

The Tortomasi Family

All Safe is a family-owned business. Tom Tortomasi Sr. founded the company in 1981 and continues to lead it today as CEO. Tom Tortomasi Jr. serves as CFO. Anne Tortomasi serves as Director. The same family that started this company is running it — and the same standards they set at the beginning are the ones we operate under now.

There is no private equity. No roll-up. No absentee ownership. When you call All Safe, you are dealing with a company that has a direct, personal stake in its reputation. That matters in a trade where corner-cutting is common.

Why Portable Extinguisher Specialists?

Most fire protection companies do multiple things: alarms, sprinklers, suppression systems, extinguishers. When extinguishers are one item on a long service list, the technician doing the work is a generalist. He knows a little about a lot. He has a checklist. He moves quickly.

Our technicians know portable fire extinguishers completely. They know the pressure tolerances, the 6-year maintenance requirements, the hydrostatic intervals, the correct agent for each hazard class, and the installation requirements for apartment stairwells, industrial facilities, commercial kitchens, and everything in between. When something is wrong with a unit, they recognize it. When a building's extinguisher mix does not match its actual hazards, they flag it.

Specialization produces better outcomes. Forty-five years of doing one thing produces expertise that a generalist cannot match.

The Problem With Competitors

We will say plainly what most vendors in our industry will not: corner-cutting is rampant in the portable fire extinguisher service business. The most common form is this: a technician arrives at a property, visually glances at each extinguisher, hangs a new annual inspection tag, and leaves. No pressure check. No internal inspection. No 6-year maintenance if it is due. No hydrostatic test if the cylinder is past its interval.

The property manager gets a certificate. The extinguishers have new tags. An audit will likely pass. But the extinguishers have not been serviced. They may not function when needed.

We do not operate this way. Every service we perform is the service we say we are performing. If a 6-year maintenance is due, we do it. If a hydrostatic test is required, we schedule it. If a unit fails inspection, we replace it. This is not a differentiator we invented for marketing purposes. It is the baseline standard we have maintained since 1981.

Our Commitment

Every service All Safe performs is the service we say we are performing. We do not hang tags on uninspected units. We do not defer required maintenance. We do not pass failing equipment. We carry written documentation for clients who need it. We use US-manufactured equipment from established brands — Amerex, Ansul, Badger, Strike First, and Buckeye.

We hold Type B, C, D, and F licenses from the California Office of the State Fire Marshal — all four license types for portable fire extinguisher contractors in California. Our technicians are licensed and trained specifically on portable extinguisher service, not cross-trained from other fire protection trades.
