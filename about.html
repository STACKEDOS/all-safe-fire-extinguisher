Why OSFM Certification Matters

In California, fire extinguisher service is regulated by the Office of the State Fire Marshal (OSFM). The OSFM is the state agency responsible for fire prevention and life safety standards, and it requires that any technician performing annual inspections, recharging, or hydrostatic testing on portable fire extinguishers hold a valid OSFM license. This is codified in the California Code of Regulations (CCR), Title 19.

Working with an OSFM-certified company is not just a best practice — it is a legal requirement. If your extinguishers are serviced by an unlicensed technician, the service tags on your equipment are invalid under California law. This creates a serious compliance problem if your business is inspected by the fire marshal, a health department inspector, or an insurance auditor. You may also find that your liability coverage is affected in the event of a fire.

All Safe technicians hold current OSFM licenses. Every inspection tag we place on your equipment includes our technician's license number as required by California law. When an inspector walks through your facility, your extinguisher tags will stand up to scrutiny.

OSFM certification isn't a marketing claim for us — it's the baseline we've operated from since 1981, because it's what California law requires and what your business deserves.
