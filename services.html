Annual Inspection

California's Title 19 regulations and NFPA 10 standards require annual fire extinguisher inspection by an OSFM-certified technician. California law requires annual inspection of every portable fire extinguisher by an OSFM-licensed contractor. All Safe holds Type B, C, D, and F licenses — all four categories issued by the California Office of the State Fire Marshal.

When required: Every 12 months — required by California Code of Regulations, Title 19

  • Pressure gauge check (must read in the operable/green zone)
  • Pull pin and tamper seal integrity
  • Discharge hose and nozzle condition
  • Cylinder body and label legibility
  • Mounting height and accessibility compliance
  • Correct extinguisher type matched to the hazard class in each space

Every inspection is tagged with the date and our technician's OSFM license number. For clients who need more than a tag — property management audits, insurance reviews, health department visits — see Written Compliance Documentation. Skipping annual inspection means your extinguishers are legally out of compliance regardless of physical condition.

Hydrostatic Testing

Hydrostatic testing for fire extinguishers is required by California law every 5 or 12 years depending on extinguisher type. Hydrostatic testing verifies cylinder structural integrity under pressure — detecting internal weaknesses invisible during standard inspection.

When required: Every 5 years (CO2 and water-based) or every 12 years (stored-pressure dry chemical) per NFPA 10

  • Cylinder pressurized to above rated working pressure to detect expansion or failure
  • Must be performed at a licensed facility with specialized equipment — not in the field
  • Cylinders that pass are date-stamped and returned to service
  • Cylinders that fail are condemned and replaced
  • Full documentation of test results provided

This is one of the most commonly skipped services in the industry. Many vendors hang a new inspection tag without conducting the required hydrostatic test. All Safe performs hydrostatic testing on schedule, every time — and never passes failing equipment.

6-Year Maintenance

The 6-year internal fire extinguisher maintenance requirement is one of the most commonly missed compliance items for California businesses. Every portable fire extinguisher must be completely disassembled, internally examined, and fully recharged every six years — a California State Fire Marshal requirement.

When required: Every 6 years per California State Fire Marshal

  • Complete discharge and disassembly of the unit
  • Internal inspection: cylinder, valve assembly, hose, dip tube, O-rings, anti-reinsert pin
  • Any worn or damaged components replaced before return to service
  • Unit recharged to factory specification and date-stamped
  • All Safe tracks 6-year dates for every client — we schedule proactively

This is one of the most frequently skipped services in the industry. A property with overdue 6-year maintenance is out of code regardless of how current the annual inspection tags appear. Missing it creates both a compliance failure and a genuine safety liability.
