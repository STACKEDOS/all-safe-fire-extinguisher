Annual Fire Extinguisher Inspection

California law requires that all fire extinguishers be inspected annually by an OSFM-certified technician. This is not optional — it is mandated under the California Code of Regulations (CCR) Title 19, and failure to comply can result in citations during fire marshal or health department inspections, and may affect your liability coverage in the event of a fire.

During an annual inspection, our certified technician examines every critical component of each extinguisher: the pressure gauge is verified to be in the green (operable) zone, the pull pin is intact and properly positioned, the tamper seal is unbroken, the discharge nozzle and hose are free of obstructions or cracks, and the cylinder body and label are free from corrosion, dents, or damage. Mounting height and accessibility are also checked to confirm compliance with NFPA 10 placement requirements.

Every inspected unit is tagged with the date of service and the technician's OSFM license number — a requirement under California law. We also provide documentation that you can keep on file for regulatory audits.

In addition to the annual inspection, California regulations require businesses to perform a monthly visual check of each unit. We can train your staff on what to look for so your monthly checks are fast and accurate.

Who needs annual fire extinguisher inspections?

Every business in California with a fire extinguisher on the premises is required to have it inspected annually. This includes restaurants, retail stores, warehouses, schools, churches, hotels, medical facilities, manufacturing plants, and construction sites. If you have an extinguisher, it must be inspected.

Fire Extinguisher Recharging & Refilling

A fire extinguisher must be recharged after every use — even a partial discharge. Once the seal is broken or the agent is partially expelled, the unit can no longer be relied upon in an emergency. Beyond post-discharge recharging, units should also be recharged if the pressure gauge drops below the green zone, if a failed inspection reveals low agent levels, or if the extinguisher was subjected to conditions that may have compromised its integrity.

Recharging is almost always more cost-effective than buying a new extinguisher. Our technicians recharge units on-site or at our Temecula facility, depending on the type and condition of the equipment. Most standard commercial units can be recharged the same day, minimizing your downtime and keeping your facility in compliance.

We recharge all common types of fire extinguishers used in Southern California commercial settings, including ABC dry chemical, CO2, wet chemical, and Class K kitchen units. Each recharged extinguisher is weighed and pressure-tested before being returned to service.

Types of extinguishers we recharge:

  • ABC dry chemical (multipurpose)
  • CO2 (carbon dioxide)
  • Wet chemical & Class K (kitchen/commercial cooking)
  • Stored pressure and cartridge-operated units
  • Halotron and clean agent extinguishers

Hydrostatic Testing

Hydrostatic testing is a pressure test performed on a fire extinguisher cylinder to verify its structural integrity. Over years of use, temperature cycling, and pressurization, the metal walls of an extinguisher cylinder can develop micro-fractures or weaknesses that are not visible to the naked eye. Hydrostatic testing pressurizes the empty cylinder to a level significantly above its rated working pressure to confirm it can safely hold its charge without risk of rupture.

NFPA 10, the National Fire Protection Association standard that governs portable fire extinguisher maintenance, establishes the testing schedule required in California. Stored-pressure dry chemical extinguishers require hydrostatic testing every 12 years. CO2 extinguishers and water-based units require testing every 5 years. Halons and certain other specialty types are tested every 12 years. All testing must be performed by a licensed facility with the appropriate equipment — it cannot be done in the field.

After testing, cylinders that pass are marked with the test date and returned to service. Cylinders that fail — typically those showing expansion beyond acceptable limits, visible damage, or leakage — must be condemned and replaced. We issue full documentation of all hydrostatic test results for your compliance records.

Hydrostatic testing schedule (NFPA 10):

  • Every 5 years: CO2 extinguishers, water and water-based agent units
  • Every 12 years: Stored-pressure dry chemical extinguishers
  • Every 12 years: Halons and certain clean agent types

Restaurant Hood Suppression Systems

Commercial kitchens present one of the highest fire risks of any business environment. Cooking equipment generates grease-laden vapors that accumulate in exhaust hoods and ducts — creating highly flammable conditions that a standard fire extinguisher cannot safely address alone. A properly installed and maintained restaurant hood suppression system is your first line of defense and is required by NFPA 96 and virtually all local fire codes for commercial cooking operations.

Under NFPA 96, restaurant hood suppression systems must be inspected every six months by a certified technician. Our inspections cover all components of the system: the agent tank and its charge level, nozzle integrity and coverage, manual pull stations, fuel shut-off linkages, and the detection and actuation system. We verify that the system will function as designed in the event of a fire and provide you with the required documentation for your health and fire department records.

Our suppression system services include:

  • Semi-annual NFPA 96 inspections (required every 6 months)
  • System recharging and agent replacement
  • Nozzle cleaning and replacement
  • Fuel shut-off and fusible link inspection
  • New system installation coordination

Fire Safety Training

Knowing where your fire extinguishers are and how to use them can mean the difference between a controlled incident and a catastrophic loss. OSHA requires employers to provide fire extinguisher training to employees who may be expected to use them, and many insurance carriers and local fire marshals look favorably on businesses that document ongoing training.

All Safe offers hands-on fire extinguisher training at your location. Our sessions cover the PASS technique (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep), how to identify the correct extinguisher type for different fire classes, when to fight a fire versus when to evacuate, and how to perform the monthly visual inspection your employees are responsible for. Sessions are tailored to your workplace and can be run for groups large or small.

Training is conducted by our experienced, OSFM-certified technicians — the same people who service your equipment. We bring practical, real-world knowledge, not scripted corporate presentations. Contact us to schedule a session for your team.

Training includes:

  • On-site, hands-on instruction at your facility
  • PASS technique and proper extinguisher operation
  • Fire class identification (A, B, C, D, K)
  • When to fight vs. when to evacuate
  • Monthly visual inspection procedures for staff
  • OSHA-aligned curriculum; documentation available upon request
