Annual Inspection

Required by California OSFM

California law requires that portable fire extinguishers be inspected annually by a licensed contractor. All Safe holds Type B, C, D, and F licenses — all four categories issued by the California Office of the State Fire Marshal. Our technicians perform a complete inspection of each unit: condition of the shell, pressure gauge, pull pin, tamper seal, discharge hose, and mounting hardware. We verify the correct extinguisher type for each hazard class present in the space.

Every inspection is documented and tagged. For clients who need more than a hanging tag for compliance purposes, we provide written service letters. See Written Compliance Documentation.

Recharging & Refilling

A discharged or partially discharged extinguisher is not a serviceable extinguisher. All Safe recharges all types of portable fire extinguishers to factory specification — dry chemical, CO2, clean agent, wet chemical, and water-based units. Recharging is required after any use, including partial discharge during a test or incident. We carry US-manufactured agents and parts from Amerex, Ansul, Badger, Strike First, and Buckeye.

Hydrostatic Testing

Required by NFPA 10 — and commonly skipped by competitors

Portable fire extinguishers must undergo hydrostatic pressure testing at regulated intervals — typically every 5 or 12 years depending on type — to verify cylinder integrity. This is a federal and state requirement under NFPA 10 and California regulations. It is also one of the most commonly skipped services in our industry.

Many vendors will hang a new inspection tag without conducting the required hydrostatic test. The extinguisher looks serviced. It is not. All Safe performs hydrostatic testing on schedule, every time. If a cylinder fails, we replace it. We do not pass failing equipment.

6-Year Maintenance

Required by California State Fire Marshal Every 6 Years

Every six years, portable fire extinguishers must undergo a full internal inspection — a requirement of the California State Fire Marshal. This goes well beyond what is performed during an annual inspection. The extinguisher must be completely emptied, internally examined, and then fully recharged.

During the 6-year service, All Safe inspects every internal component: the cylinder interior, valve assembly, hose, dip tube, O-rings, and anti-reinsert pin. Each part is assessed for wear, corrosion, or damage. The cylinder is then marked with a date stamp confirming when the service was performed. Any component that does not meet standard is replaced before the unit is returned to service.

This is one of the most frequently skipped services in the industry. A property that has not received its required 6-year maintenance is out of code — regardless of how current the annual inspection tags appear. Missing this service puts your property out of compliance and creates a genuine safety liability.

All Safe tracks 6-year maintenance dates for every client we serve. We schedule proactively and notify you when service is due — so your properties never fall out of compliance without warning.

Apartment Building Second-Landing Installs

A Specialized Niche — We Do This Better Than Anyone

Many apartment buildings throughout Southern California were built without portable fire extinguisher coverage on second-floor stairwell landings. As compliance requirements have increased, property owners are now required to install extinguishers and cabinets retroactively on these second landings.

All Safe specializes in this exact service. We assess each building's stairwell configuration, select the appropriate cabinet and extinguisher combination for the outdoor or semi-exposed environment, and install to current California code requirements. We have completed this service across hundreds of properties for the largest property management companies in the country.

If your apartment portfolio has buildings that were originally constructed without second-floor extinguisher coverage, contact us. This is exactly what we do.

Written Compliance Documentation

For most routine compliance purposes, the annual inspection tag attached to each extinguisher is sufficient. But when a compliance audit, insurance review, or regulatory inspection requires a full service record, a tag is not enough.

All Safe provides written service documentation for clients who need it — a formal letter detailing what was inspected, what services were performed, what was replaced, and when. This documentation is used by property management companies, school districts, corporate facilities teams, and others during portfolio-level audits and third-party compliance reviews.

If your organization requires written documentation in addition to or instead of standard tagging, request it when you contact us.
