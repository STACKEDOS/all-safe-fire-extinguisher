Fire Extinguisher Service for Every Type of Business

California law requires every commercial business to maintain properly inspected and serviced fire extinguishers — no matter the industry. From health department compliance in restaurant kitchens to OSHA requirements on active construction sites, All Safe Fire Extinguisher Co. has been keeping Southern California businesses code-compliant and protected since 1981.

Every industry has unique fire hazards, different extinguisher requirements, and specific inspection schedules. Below is a breakdown of the industries we most commonly serve — and exactly what fire safety compliance looks like for each one.

🍳

Restaurants & Commercial Kitchens

Commercial kitchens are among the highest fire-risk environments in any business category. Open flames, deep fryers loaded with grease, and exhaust hoods packed with residue create conditions where a small incident can become a catastrophic blaze within seconds. For this reason, restaurants face the most stringent fire extinguisher and suppression requirements of any industry.

Class K Extinguishers — Non-Negotiable for Kitchens

California fire code — and NFPA 96 (Standard for Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations) — requires that any commercial cooking operation using vegetable or animal oils must have a Class K fire extinguisher mounted within 30 feet of the cooking equipment. Class K agents are specifically formulated to suppress grease fires by saponifying the oil (turning it into a soapy foam), which standard ABC extinguishers cannot reliably do.

All Safe supplies, installs, and services Class K extinguishers and ensures they are mounted, tagged, and ready for health department inspection.

Hood Suppression Systems

Beyond portable extinguishers, NFPA 96 also mandates a fixed automatic suppression system (typically Ansul or equivalent) within the hood above cooking surfaces. These systems must be inspected every 6 months by a certified technician — twice the frequency of most other extinguisher inspections. All Safe is certified to perform these semi-annual hood suppression inspections throughout Southern California.

Health Department & Fire Marshal Compliance

Health department inspectors and local fire marshals check for current inspection tags, proper extinguisher placement, and visible access — all on every routine visit. An expired tag or missing extinguisher can mean a failed inspection and a costly shutdown. We keep your paperwork current and your extinguishers in spec so you never fail a surprise inspection.

Key Compliance Points for Restaurants:
  • Class K extinguisher within 30 ft of grease cooking surfaces
  • Hood suppression system — semi-annual inspection (every 6 months)
  • Portable extinguishers — annual inspection minimum
  • NFPA 96 and California Health & Safety Code compliance
  • Current inspection tags visible at all times

Warehouses & Manufacturing Facilities

Large-footprint warehouses and manufacturing plants present a unique set of fire safety challenges: high ceilings, dense racking systems, flammable materials, and operational hazards like forklifts and chemical storage. NFPA 10 governs the placement and type of portable fire extinguishers in these environments, and the rules are more demanding than those for a typical office or retail space.

High-Hazard Classification & Extinguisher Rating

Most warehouses and manufacturing facilities are classified as Extra Hazard (Group 1 or 2) under NFPA 10, meaning they require higher-rated extinguishers spaced closer together than in ordinary-hazard environments. A minimum 4-A:80-B:C rating is common, and in areas with significant flammable liquid storage, even higher ratings may be required. All Safe conducts a full site assessment to determine the correct number, type, and placement of extinguishers for your specific facility.

Forklift Areas & Charging Stations

Electric forklift charging stations create a lithium-ion or lead-acid battery fire hazard that requires specific attention. Propane-powered forklifts add a Class B flammable gas risk. We identify these zones during our site assessment and ensure appropriate coverage is in place.

Chemical Storage Areas

Any area where flammable liquids, solvents, or hazardous materials are stored requires Class B-rated extinguishers — and in some cases, CO₂ or dry chemical units — at prescribed distances. We help you map your chemical storage against NFPA 10 travel-distance requirements so every hazard zone is covered.

Key Compliance Points for Warehouses & Manufacturing:
  • Extra-hazard classification — higher-rated extinguishers required
  • NFPA 10 travel distance requirements (typically 75 ft max)
  • Class B coverage for flammable liquid and chemical storage areas
  • Annual inspection — more frequent if operationally required
  • Monthly visual inspections by designated employee

Schools & Educational Facilities

Schools, community colleges, and childcare facilities face strict fire safety oversight because of high occupant loads and the presence of children and young adults. California's Office of the State Fire Marshal (OSFM) and local fire marshals conduct regular inspections of educational campuses, and fire extinguisher compliance is always on the checklist.

Strict Fire Marshal Inspections

Educational facilities in California are subject to annual fire inspections by the local authority having jurisdiction (AHJ). Inspectors check that all extinguishers are current on their annual service, properly mounted at the correct height, unobstructed, and equipped with a current inspection tag. All Safe provides the documentation and tagging to pass these inspections with confidence.

Campus-Wide Coverage

A typical K–12 campus includes classrooms, science labs (with chemical hazards), cafeteria kitchens, maintenance areas, and gymnasiums — each with different fire hazard profiles. We assess every area and ensure the right extinguisher types are placed throughout the campus, from standard ABC units in hallways to Class K in the cafeteria kitchen.

Staff Training Value

All Safe offers hands-on fire extinguisher training for school staff. In an emergency, a trained employee can suppress a small fire before it spreads — protecting students and giving evacuation time. We offer training in conjunction with our inspection services.

Key Compliance Points for Schools:
  • Annual OSFM / fire marshal inspection compliance
  • Cafeteria kitchens require Class K coverage
  • Science labs may require CO₂ or specialized agents
  • All units must be mounted at correct height and clearly visible
  • Staff training recommended and may be required by AHJ

Construction Sites

Active construction sites are among the most fire-hazardous work environments — with open flame work (welding, cutting, torching), flammable materials (lumber, insulation, adhesives, fuels), temporary electrical installations, and a constantly changing site layout. OSHA and California fire code impose specific requirements for fire extinguisher availability on construction sites.

OSHA Requirements — 29 CFR 1926.150

OSHA's construction safety standard (29 CFR 1926.150) requires that a fire extinguisher rated not less than 2-A be provided for each 3,000 square feet of protected building area, and no point of a building shall be more than 100 feet from an extinguisher. In areas where flammable or combustible liquids are present, additional Class B extinguishers must be provided. All Safe helps contractors meet these requirements with properly rated and maintained units.

Temporary Fire Protection

Many construction projects require temporary fire protection throughout the build — before the permanent sprinkler and suppression systems are installed and operational. We supply, service, and track temporary extinguishers for the duration of the project, updating placement as the site evolves.

Hot Work Areas

Any area where welding, cutting, brazing, or soldering occurs must have a fire extinguisher immediately available at the work location. We ensure your hot-work crews are always equipped and that extinguishers are inspected regularly throughout the project.

Key Compliance Points for Construction Sites:
  • OSHA 29 CFR 1926.150 — one 2-A extinguisher per 3,000 sq ft
  • Maximum 100-ft travel distance to nearest extinguisher
  • Class B extinguishers required near fuel and flammable liquid storage
  • Extinguisher at every hot-work location
  • Monthly inspections; annual service for long-duration projects

Office Buildings & Retail Spaces

While offices and retail stores have lower inherent fire risk than restaurants or warehouses, California fire code still requires properly maintained extinguishers — and local fire marshals inspect these properties routinely. Non-compliance can result in fines and, more importantly, leaves employees and customers unprotected.

Standard ABC Coverage

Most office and retail environments require standard ABC dry chemical extinguishers, which handle ordinary combustibles (paper, wood, fabric), flammable liquids, and electrical fires. NFPA 10 specifies placement at no more than 75 feet of travel distance for ordinary-hazard occupancies, with units mounted 3.5 to 5 feet off the floor.

Break Rooms & Kitchenettes

Office kitchenettes and break rooms with microwaves, toasters, and small appliances are a common ignition source in commercial buildings. We assess these areas and ensure appropriate coverage — and can recommend a Class K unit if cooking appliances are present.

Employee Training

OSHA and California fire code encourage (and in some cases require) that employees know how to use a fire extinguisher. All Safe offers simple, practical PASS method training that takes less than 30 minutes and can be delivered on-site during or after your annual inspection.

Key Compliance Points for Offices & Retail:
  • ABC extinguishers — 75-ft max travel distance (ordinary hazard)
  • Mounted 3.5 to 5 ft from floor; unobstructed and visible
  • Annual inspection and service by OSFM-certified technician
  • Monthly visual checks by designated employee
  • Employee training — PASS method recommended

HOAs & Property Management Companies

Homeowners associations and property management companies are responsible for fire extinguisher compliance across common areas, garages, laundry rooms, clubhouses, and multi-unit residential buildings. Managing compliance across dozens — or hundreds — of extinguisher locations at multiple properties is complex. All Safe makes it simple.

Multi-Unit & Common Area Requirements

California Health & Safety Code requires fire extinguishers in the common areas of multi-unit residential buildings — including apartment complexes, condominiums, and gated communities. This includes parking garages, laundry facilities, mail rooms, pool equipment areas, and any common-area kitchen or club room.

Parking Garage Requirements

Attached or enclosed parking structures require extinguishers rated for Class B hazards (flammable liquids from vehicles) in addition to ordinary Class A hazards. NFPA 10 placement requirements apply, and units must be clearly marked, unobstructed, and within travel distance requirements. We assess your garage layout and install units correctly from the start.

Tracking Multiple Properties & Units

Property managers often oversee extinguishers at many locations simultaneously. All Safe maintains detailed service records for every extinguisher we service, organized by property. We provide reminder notifications when annual inspections are due — so nothing slips through the cracks and you're never caught with expired tags during a fire marshal visit.

Streamlined Compliance Across Your Portfolio

We work with property management companies to schedule inspections efficiently — batching multiple properties into a single service route where possible, minimizing disruption to residents, and providing consolidated documentation for your compliance records.

Key Compliance Points for HOAs & Property Managers:
  • Required in all common areas — laundry, garages, clubhouses, mail rooms
  • Class B coverage in parking garages and near vehicle charging stations
  • Class K in any common-area kitchen with cooking appliances
  • Annual inspection by OSFM-certified technician
  • Consolidated service records available for all managed properties
