Fire Extinguisher Service & Inspection FAQ — California Requirements & Compliance

Answers to the most common questions we hear from property managers and business owners across Southern California about fire extinguisher inspection, recharging, and compliance requirements.

About Inspections & Compliance

How often do fire extinguishers need to be inspected in California?

California requires fire extinguishers to be inspected at multiple intervals:

  • Monthly visual check — performed by a designated employee; confirms location, pressure gauge in green zone, pin and seal intact, no physical damage
  • Annual licensed inspection — required by CCR Title 19; must be performed by an OSFM-certified technician; results in a dated, signed inspection tag
  • 6-year internal examination — required by California State Fire Marshal for stored-pressure dry chemical extinguishers; full disassembly and internal inspection
  • Hydrostatic testing — every 5 or 12 years depending on extinguisher type per NFPA 10; verifies cylinder structural integrity

What happens during a fire extinguisher inspection?

Our OSFM-certified technician performs a methodical check of every critical component:

  • Pressure gauge — must read in the operable (green) zone; red zone means immediate recharge required
  • Pull pin — fully inserted and properly positioned; a missing or partially seated pin means the unit cannot be reliably activated
  • Tamper seal — confirms the unit has not been discharged or tampered with since last inspection
  • Discharge nozzle and hose — checked for obstructions, cracks, or deterioration
  • Cylinder body and label — examined for corrosion, dents, or damage; illegible labels are a compliance failure under NFPA 10
  • Mounting and accessibility — correct height, clearly visible, unobstructed per NFPA 10 requirements

At the conclusion, we affix a dated tag with our technician's OSFM license number as required by California law.

Do I need OSFM-certified technicians for fire extinguisher service in California?

Yes. California Code of Regulations Title 19 requires that annual inspections, recharging, and hydrostatic testing be performed by OSFM-licensed technicians. Service by an unlicensed technician produces legally invalid inspection tags — a compliance failure regardless of the extinguisher's physical condition. This matters during fire marshal inspections, health department visits, and insurance carrier reviews.

All Safe Fire Extinguisher Company technicians are fully licensed by the California OSFM. Every inspection tag we affix includes the technician's OSFM license number as required by California law.

What is hydrostatic testing for fire extinguishers?

Hydrostatic testing pressurizes an empty cylinder to above its rated working pressure to verify structural integrity. Over years of use, metal cylinders can develop micro-fractures or weakening invisible during standard inspection. Required intervals under NFPA 10:

  • CO2 and water-based extinguishers — every 5 years
  • Stored-pressure dry chemical extinguishers — every 12 years

Hydrostatic testing must be performed at a licensed facility — it cannot be done in the field. Extinguishers past their due date are technically out of service. Cylinders that fail are condemned and replaced; those that pass are date-stamped and returned to service.

How many fire extinguishers does my building need?

The number and type depends on your facility's size, layout, and hazard class. Under NFPA 10, extinguishers must be placed so the maximum travel distance from any point does not exceed 75 feet for Class A hazards or 50 feet for Class B hazards. Restaurants and commercial kitchens also require a Class K extinguisher within 30 feet of cooking equipment. Most commercial spaces need 2–4 units minimum, varying by square footage. Contact us and we'll assess your specific space.

What type of fire extinguisher do I need for my business?

Fire extinguishers are classified by the fire types they fight. The most common commercial extinguisher is the ABC dry chemical (multipurpose) unit, which covers Class A (ordinary combustibles), B (flammable liquids), and C (energized electrical equipment) fires. It's appropriate for most offices, retail, warehouses, and light manufacturing. Specialty situations require different types: Class K units for commercial kitchens, CO2 or clean agent for server rooms (no residue), and Class D for combustible metal environments. All Safe assesses your operations and hazard profile to recommend correctly.

How long does a fire extinguisher last?

A well-maintained fire extinguisher can remain in service for 10–12 years or longer. Service life depends on passing all required annual inspections, hydrostatic testing at the correct intervals, and the absence of significant physical damage. An extinguisher that fails hydrostatic testing or shows structural damage must be replaced regardless of age. Most manufacturers recommend replacement after 12 years as a general guideline. The inspection and testing schedule exists precisely to make that determination accurately.

OSFM, Title 19 & California-Specific Requirements

What does OSFM-certified mean for fire extinguisher service in California?

OSFM stands for California Office of the State Fire Marshal. State law requires that anyone performing fire extinguisher service, inspection, or recharging in California hold an active OSFM license. All Safe technicians are OSFM-certified under Type B, C, D, and F licenses — covering the full range of portable extinguishers. When you hire an uncertified vendor, your compliance documentation has no legal standing.

What is Title 19 and how does it affect fire extinguisher service in California?

Title 19 is California's Code of Regulations governing fire protection equipment, including portable fire extinguishers. It requires annual inspection by a licensed technician, 6-year internal maintenance, and hydrostatic testing on schedule. Non-compliance can result in failed fire inspections, insurance issues, and liability exposure. All Safe performs all fire extinguisher service to Title 19 standard and provides written documentation for your records.

Do restaurants need special fire extinguisher service?

Yes. Commercial kitchens require Class K fire extinguishers specifically rated for cooking oil fires. These must be inspected annually and are subject to the same Title 19 and NFPA 10 requirements as other extinguisher types. All Safe services Class K extinguishers for restaurants, cafeterias, and food production facilities throughout Southern California.

Do apartment buildings need annual fire extinguisher inspection?

Yes. California Health & Safety Code and Title 19 require all residential buildings — including apartments and multifamily complexes — to have fire extinguishers inspected annually by an OSFM-certified technician. All Safe handles fire extinguisher service and inspection for apartment buildings of every size across Southern California, including second-landing installs required by code.
